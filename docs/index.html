Core Capabilities

Two modes.
One engine.

Nature Risk automatically routes every query to the correct toolchain — inland hydrological or coastal marine — based on your input coordinates.

🌿

Inland Hydrological Intelligence

Trace flow paths, delineate catchments, and predict how tree planting, peat restoration, and leaky dams reduce peak flood height at a target asset — using 1m EA LIDAR and OS Open Rivers.

  • 1m-resolution LIDAR catchment tracing
  • Peat, woodland & leaky dam simulation
  • Peak flow reduction in metres + hours
  • UKCP18 25/50-year projections
🌊

Coastal Marine Intelligence

Model wave attenuation from oyster reefs, seagrass meadows, and saltmarsh against UKHO bathymetry and NTSLF tidal data to quantify the reduction in storm surge height at onshore infrastructure.

  • UKHO bathymetry wave profile analysis
  • Reef, seagrass & saltmarsh modelling
  • Wave energy & surge reduction (%)
  • 25-year erosion delta under sea-level rise
🧠

Triage & Advisory Logic

Claude AI synthesises physics engine outputs into plain-English investment narratives, validates intervention placements in real time, and flags scale warnings before analysis begins.

  • Automatic Inland / Coastal / Mixed routing
  • Real-time soil & substrate suitability check
  • Minimum viable area scale warnings
  • One-click Board-ready PDF export
Interaction Design

Co-Pilot + Map.
Three steps.

A split-screen interface: conversational AI on the left, live GIS visualisation on the right. Both update in real time as the agent works through each data source.

Step 01

Draw & Drop

Draw a polygon around a proposed wetland, peatland, or woodland, or drop a pin directly on the corporate asset you want to protect.

Mapbox GL polygon draw tool — minimum 0.5 ha polygon

Step 02

Agent Analyses

The Action Stream shows live progress: catchment delineation via EA LIDAR, soil validation via BGS Soilscapes, rainfall from Met Office UKCP18 — all in parallel.

End-to-end latency target: under 2 minutes

Step 03

Review & Export

Risk Delta dial, hydrograph, confidence score, and Opportunity Zone cards render in the chat pane. Export a Board-ready PDF with all citations and disclaimers in one click.

Output: peak flow reduction (m), delay (hrs), uncertainty (%)

Step 01

Draw & Drop

Draw a polygon for a proposed oyster reef, seagrass meadow, or saltmarsh, then drop a pin on the coastal asset — sea wall, port, road — you want to protect.

Auto-detects coastal mode from coordinates within 5 km of coast

Step 02

Agent Analyses

UKHO bathymetry profile, NTSLF tidal data, Met Office WaveNet, and Cefas/EA saltmarsh extents are queried. WASM wave energy engine computes attenuation.

UKCP18 sea-level rise scenarios applied; never assumes static baseline

Step 03

Review & Export

Wave energy reduction (%), storm surge height reduction (m), and 25-year erosion delta surface in the chat pane alongside habitat maturation timeline and confidence badge.

Includes mandatory dynamic morphology disclosure

Technical Architecture

Static. Fast.
Deterministic.

Zero backend infrastructure. All computation happens at the edge or client-side — a Cloudflare Worker proxies UK government APIs, a Rust WASM engine handles all physics, and Claude handles narrative.

Safety & Constraints

Built-in guardrails,
not afterthoughts.

Every output is bounded by hard technical and regulatory constraints defined before a single line of analysis runs.

🔓

Not an Engineer Rule

Every output carries a mandatory disclaimer. Results are directional pre-feasibility proxies — never a substitute for a certified Flood Risk Assessment or regulated EIA.

🚫

Zero Hallucination on Geospatial Data

If any primary data API fails, the agent surfaces a DATA_UNAVAILABLE error and stops. It never interpolates or fabricates topographic or bathymetric values.

⚙

Deterministic Physics Engine

All quantitative calculations run in a Rust WASM engine, not the LLM. Identical inputs always produce identical outputs. The LLM synthesises — it does not calculate.

💳

FCA Compliance

The agent does not provide regulated financial advice, guarantee insurance premium reductions, or certify carbon credit yields for regulatory submission.

🎯

Confidence Scoring (Mandatory)

Every output includes a visible Low / Medium / High confidence level with cited data sources, resolution, recency, and a quantified uncertainty range (e.g., ±3%).

🌊

Dynamic Morphology Disclosure

Coastal outputs explicitly state habitat maturation timelines, inter-annual storm variability (±20%), and confirm that UKCP18 sea-level rise is incorporated.
